Recycling Plastic

Plastic materials are ubiquitous in the 21st century, with 8.3 billion metric tons produced in 70 years. Unfortunately, only 9% of this plastic waste has been recycled.

Statistics in Nigeria

  • Plastics constitute about 15-17% of municipal solid waste in Nigeria.
  • Nigeria generates approximately 2.5 million tons of plastic waste annually, with a recycling rate of less than 10%.

Benefits of Recycling Plastic

  • Reduces the need for raw materials, conserving resources and energy.
  • Decreases the amount of plastic waste in landfills and oceans.
  • Helps in reducing greenhouse gas emissions from plastic production.
  • Supports the recycling industry and creates jobs.

Types of Plastics

Plastics are categorized by resin types:

  • PETE (1): Commonly recycled for bottles and containers.
  • HDPE (2): Used for sturdier products like detergent bottles.
  • PVC (3): Rarely recycled due to toxicity.
  • LDPE (4): Used for containers and plastic bags; recycling is limited.
  • PP (5): Found in caps and food containers; often rejected by recyclers.
  • PS (6): Used for disposable containers; rarely recycled.
  • Other (7): Includes mixed and non-recyclable plastics.

Challenges in Plastic Recycling

Recycling plastic is complex due to contamination, mixed materials, and varying recyclability. Effective recycling requires awareness and proper sorting of plastics to prevent contamination.

Tips for Recycling Plastic at Home

  • Remove non-recyclable parts like metal caps and labels.
  • Don't bag plastics; place them directly in recycling bins.
  • Check labels for biodegradable plastics.
  • Organize a recycling station at home for easier sorting.
  • Repurpose plastics creatively to reduce waste.
  • Spread awareness and encourage community participation.
  • Reduce plastic use by opting for reusable alternatives.

Plastic Recycling Process

  1. Collection + Distribution: Collecting plastics from various sources.
  2. Sorting + Categorizing: Sorting plastics by type and properties.
  3. Washing: Removing impurities such as labels and food residues.
  4. Shredding: Breaking plastics into smaller pieces for easier processing.
  5. Identification + Separation: Classifying plastics by density and thickness.
  6. Extruding + Compounding: Melting and forming plastic into pellets for reuse.

Ways to Recycle Plastic at Home

Basic Recycling

  • Separation: Separate plastics from other waste and follow local recycling guidelines.
  • Rinsing and Cleaning: Clean plastic containers to remove food residues.
  • Proper Disposal: Place plastics in designated recycling bins.

Creative Recycling

  • DIY Plastic Crafts: Transform plastic containers into decorative items.
  • Plastic Bag Repurposing: Create reusable items from plastic bags.
  • Garden Projects: Use plastic bottles as planters or irrigation systems.
  • Eco-Brick Building: Fill bottles with plastic waste to create eco-bricks for construction.
